About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineering undergraduate with hands-on experience in regulated medical device development, test engineering, and design verification. I am especially drawn to hands-on engineering work that involves building, testing, and refining systems in environments where precision and reliability are critical.

My interest in engineering started early. In middle school, I began building and repairing computers — diagnosing hardware failures, swapping components, and learning how systems interact at both a physical and logical level. That curiosity naturally expanded into a broader interest in how complex systems are designed, tested, and improved.

Outside of academics and work, I am a car enthusiast who enjoys understanding vehicles beyond the surface level — from performance engineering to mechanical design decisions. This hands-on mindset carries into my engineering work: I enjoy building things, breaking them, understanding why they fail, and making them better. Looking ahead, I want to continue developing my skills within the biomedical device industry while exploring opportunities to apply engineering principles in the automotive space as well.

Projects

Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) Investigations

Scope / Challenge: Supported a Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) investigation within a regulated medical device quality system to address a product performance issue. The goal was to identify root causes, support effective corrective actions, and maintain full documentation and traceability throughout the process.

Actions:
  • Analyzed test data and reviewed failure trends to support the investigation
  • Worked with engineering, manufacturing, and quality teams to evaluate possible causes
  • Contributed technical input during root-cause analysis and corrective action discussions
  • Supported verification of corrective actions through review of test results and documented evidence
  • Assisted with quality system documentation to justify technical decisions and support traceability
Results:
  • Helped strengthen confidence that corrective actions addressed root causes rather than symptoms
  • Contributed to improved product performance and a more structured investigation process
  • Reinforced the value of evidence-based decision making in a regulated environment

Test Fixtures for ISO-10555 Test Methods

Scope / Challenge: Designed and built custom test fixtures to support verification testing of intravascular catheters in accordance with ISO 10555 requirements. The project focused on reducing operator variability and improving measurement repeatability.

Actions:
  • Translated ISO-based test requirements into physical hardware designs
  • Prototyped and iterated on fixture designs for alignment and measurement accuracy
  • Collaborated with engineering and quality teams to validate fixture performance
  • Applied root-cause thinking when addressing test variability issues
Results:
  • Delivered test fixtures that improved repeatability in catheter verification testing
  • Reduced operator-dependent measurement variation
  • Supported compliant testing aligned with ISO 10555 requirements
